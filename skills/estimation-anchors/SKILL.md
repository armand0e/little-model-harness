# Estimation Anchors (Number Reference)

Memorized quantities for Fermi estimates (see fermi-estimation for the method) and for catching absurd numbers. All approximate, mid-2020s.

## Powers of ten intuition

10³ thousand, 10⁶ million, 10⁹ billion, 10¹² trillion. 2¹⁰ ≈ 10³ (so 2²⁰≈10⁶, 2³⁰≈10⁹). A million seconds ≈ 11.6 days; a billion seconds ≈ 32 years; a trillion seconds ≈ 32,000 years — the gap between million and billion is vast, a common intuition failure.

## People

World 8.1B. India 1.43B, China 1.41B, US 335M, Indonesia 275M, Pakistan 240M, Brazil 215M, Nigeria 220M, Russia 145M, Japan 124M, Germany 84M, UK/France 68M, Canada 40M, Australia 26M. US households ~130M @ ~2.5 people. Births worldwide ~130M/yr; deaths ~60M/yr. Humans ever lived ~110B.

## Time & human rates

Lifespan ~80 yr ≈ 29,000 days ≈ 700,000 h. Waking adult hours ~250k. Working year ~2,000 h. Heartbeat ~10⁵/day. Reading ~250 wpm; speech ~130 wpm; typing ~40 wpm. Sleep ~1/3 of life.

## Distance & speed

Earth circumference 40,000 km; radius 6,371 km. Walking 5 km/h; running ~10–20 km/h; car highway ~100 km/h; jet ~900 km/h; sound 343 m/s; light 3×10⁸ m/s. Earth–Moon 384,000 km; Earth–Sun 150M km (8.3 light-min). City block ~100 m; building floor ~3 m.

World GDP ~$105T. US ~$28T, China ~$18T, Japan/Germany ~$4T. US GDP/capita ~$83k; world average ~$13k. US median household income ~$75k/yr; median home ~$420k. US federal budget ~$6T. Minimum-wage-ish ~$15/h → ~$30k/yr full time. Gallon of gas ~$3.5; kWh ~$0.15; movie ticket ~$13; coffee ~$5. Big Mac ~$5. Billionaire = 10⁹; a stack of that many dollar bills ~ 100+ km tall.

## Data & compute

Byte=char; KB~1,000; page of text ~2–4 KB; book ~1 MB; photo ~3 MB; song ~5 MB; MP3 minute ~1 MB; HD movie ~4 GB; hour HD video ~3 GB. Phone storage 128–512 GB; laptop RAM 8–32 GB; disk 1 TB. Human genome ~750 MB. Internet traffic: exabytes/day. Typing → ~10 bytes/s; broadband ~100 Mbps.

## Physical stuff

Water 1 kg/L; 1 m³ = 1000 kg = 1000 L. Human ~70 kg, ~1.7 m. Car ~1,500 kg. Bathtub ~150 L; Olympic pool ~2.5M L. Paper sheet ~5 g, 0.1 mm thick. Apple ~150 g. Bag of sugar 1 kg. Air ~1.2 kg/m³ (a room's air weighs ~50 kg). 

## Energy & power

Human diet ~2,000 kcal/day ≈ 100 W average (a human ≈ a lightbulb). LED bulb 10 W; laptop 50 W; kettle 2 kW; home avg ~1 kW (~30 kWh/day, US); car engine ~100 kW (~130 hp). Gasoline ~34 MJ/L. AA battery ~10 kJ.

## Biology & scale

Cell ~10 µm; bacterium ~1 µm; virus ~100 nm; atom ~0.1 nm; DNA width ~2 nm. Human ~37 trillion cells, ~86B neurons. Ant ~5 mg; blue whale ~150 t (largest animal ever). Tree can be ~100 m tall. Bacteria on Earth outnumber stars in the observable universe (~10³⁰ vs ~10²²).

## Using anchors to catch lies

Any specific number can be vetted: "a company with 5 billion daily users" (> world population → false); "drove 2,000 km in 3 hours" (667 km/h → impossible by car); "the file is 40 GB of plain text" (≈ 40,000 books → implausible for a text file). Whenever a number surprises you, reach for the nearest anchor and divide.
