# Biology Essentials

## Reliable workflow

1. Identify the biological level: molecule, cell, tissue, organism, population, or ecosystem. Keep claims at the level the evidence supports.
2. Separate mechanism, observation, and inference. A plausible mechanism does not prove a clinical effect, and a population association does not diagnose an individual.
3. Define species/population, life stage, environment, dose, time scale, and comparison. These conditions often reverse broad claims.
4. Prefer converging evidence and current authoritative sources for health claims. Report effect size, baseline risk, uncertainty, and applicability—not only a mechanism or headline.
5. Check for exceptions, tradeoffs, and alternative explanations, then state the answer at the appropriate confidence.

For personal or urgent health questions, give general information, identify red flags, and direct the person to appropriate medical or emergency help rather than improvising a diagnosis or dose.

## Cells & molecules of life

- All life is cells: bacteria are single cells without a nucleus (prokaryotes); plants/animals/fungi have nucleated cells (eukaryotes). A human is ~30–40 trillion cells, plus a comparable number of resident bacteria (mostly gut — mostly beneficial).
- **DNA** is the instruction book: same DNA in nearly every cell of a body; genes are recipes for proteins; proteins do the work (enzymes, structure, signaling). Flow: DNA → RNA → protein. Human genome ≈ 3 billion base pairs, ~20,000 protein-coding genes. Humans share ~99.9% of DNA with each other, ~98%+ with chimps.
- Energy: mitochondria burn glucose + O₂ → ATP + CO₂ + H₂O (why you inhale O₂ and exhale CO₂). Plants run photosynthesis: CO₂ + H₂O + light → glucose + O₂ (they make food from air and light; soil supplies water and minerals, not the carbon — a tree's mass comes mostly from CO₂).

## Genetics quick kit

- Humans usually carry two copies of each autosomal gene, one inherited from each parent. Dominant/recessive inheritance is a useful model for some single-gene variants, not a rule for every trait. Eye color, height, and most common traits are polygenic and environmentally influenced; do not force them into a one-gene Punnett square.
- Typical human chromosomal development is often summarized as XX and XY, with the egg contributing X and the sperm X or Y. Real sex development also includes chromosomal, gonadal, hormonal, and anatomical variation. X-linked recessive variants are more likely to be expressed in people with one X chromosome.
- Heritable = influenced by genes, NOT "fixed at birth"; heritability is a population statistic, not a personal destiny.

## Evolution (the actual theory, not the caricature)

- Variation (mutation, recombination) + differential reproduction (selection) + inheritance → populations change. Individuals don't evolve; populations do, across generations.
- "Survival of the fittest" = best-REPRODUCING in that environment, not strongest. Evolution has no goals, no ladder; humans didn't evolve "from chimps" — we share a common ancestor (~6–7 Mya).
- **Antibiotic resistance** is evolution observable in real time. Antibiotics do not treat viruses; use them only when prescribed, follow the current prescription and clinician's instructions, do not share leftovers, and seek advice rather than extending or stopping a course on your own.
- Timescales: life ~3.8 billion yr; animals ~600 Myr; dinosaurs died 66 Mya; Homo sapiens ~300,000 yr; agriculture ~12,000 yr. Humans and (non-avian) dinosaurs never coexisted — birds ARE surviving dinosaurs.

## Human body numbers

Typical resting adult anchors include heart rate roughly 60–100 beats/min, breathing about 12–20/min, about 5 L of blood, and core temperature near 37°C. These ranges vary with age, measurement site, fitness, pregnancy, medication, altitude, and illness and are not diagnostic cutoffs by themselves. Brain mass is about 1.4 kg and the brain uses roughly 20% of resting energy; adult skeletons commonly have 206 bones. The "people use only 10% of the brain" claim is false.

## Health-claim sanity kit

- **Vaccines** expose the immune system to an antigen or genetic instructions for an antigen without producing the full target disease in the ordinary way. Benefits, schedules, contraindications, and adverse effects are vaccine-specific; evaluate them with current public-health and clinical guidance. Large bodies of evidence do not support a causal link between routine vaccination and autism.
- **Antibiotics** target susceptible bacteria, not viruses. Unnecessary use increases side effects and selection for resistance.
- **Evidence hierarchy**: mechanistic plausibility and animal studies can motivate a hypothesis; human clinical decisions need relevant trials, systematic evidence, surveillance, and applicability to the patient.
- **Absolute risk matters**: translate relative claims into baseline and absolute numbers. "Doubles risk" can mean 1 in a million to 2 in a million.
- **Red flags for weak claims**: testimonials instead of comparison groups, one nutrient blamed for everything, detox language without a named substance and dose, guaranteed cures, suppressed-secret narratives, or sales urgency.

Do not convert these anchors into diagnosis. Symptoms such as trouble breathing, chest pain, stroke signs, severe allergic reaction, poisoning, major bleeding, or altered consciousness require urgent local medical help.
